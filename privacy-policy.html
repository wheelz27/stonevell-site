01 Who We Are

Stonevell is a digital services company based in Northern Nevada serving customers in Reno, Sparks, Carson City, and remotely. We provide device repair, managed IT, cybersecurity, web presence services, AI automation, and remote computer support under the brand name "Stonevell" and the tagline "Your Digital Fortress."

This Privacy Policy describes how Stonevell ("we," "us," "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects information when you visit our website (stonevell.com), use our services, submit forms, or interact with us through any channel.

02 Information We Collect

Information you provide directly:

  • Name, email address, phone number, and city/state when you submit a contact or intake form
  • Device type, operating system, and description of technical issues submitted through our remote support intake form
  • Any files, screenshots, or documents you voluntarily share with us during a support engagement
  • Communication content from emails, phone calls, or text messages you send to us
  • Billing and payment information if you purchase a service (processed through third-party payment processors — we do not store full payment card numbers)

Information collected automatically:

  • IP address, browser type, operating system, device type, and screen resolution
  • Pages visited, time spent on pages, referring URLs, and click behavior
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (see Section 5 below)

Information we do not collect:

  • We do not intentionally access, copy, or store your passwords, personal files, financial records, or other sensitive personal data unless you specifically request assistance with those items during a support session
  • We do not purchase or obtain personal data from third-party data brokers

03 How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Respond to your inquiries and provide requested services
  • Schedule, conduct, and follow up on remote and in-person support sessions
  • Diagnose and resolve technical issues on your devices
  • Process payments and send invoices or receipts
  • Send service-related communications (appointment confirmations, follow-up instructions, project updates)
  • Improve our website, services, and customer experience
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your personal information to anyone. We do not use your information for automated decision-making or profiling.

04 Remote Support Sessions

Remote support sessions require special attention because they involve direct access to your device. Here is how we handle your privacy during remote sessions:

  • Consent-based access. We only connect to your device after you explicitly authorize the session by running the connection client and approving access. You can revoke access and end the session at any time.
  • Visible activity. Everything we do during a remote session is visible on your screen in real time. There is no hidden or background access.
  • No recording by default. We do not record remote support sessions unless we explicitly disclose it to you and receive your consent before the session begins.
  • No data harvesting. We do not browse, access, copy, or extract personal files, documents, photos, or credentials from your device during a session unless you specifically request assistance with those items.
  • Session termination. Once the session ends, our remote access is terminated. We do not maintain persistent or unattended access to your device unless a separate unattended access agreement is in place for managed IT customers.

For managed IT customers with ongoing unattended access agreements, access terms are defined in your service agreement and are separate from one-time remote support sessions.

05 Cookies & Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to understand how visitors interact with the site. This may include:

  • Essential cookies required for the website to function (for example, form submissions)
  • Analytics cookies that help us understand traffic patterns, page views, and user behavior in aggregate (for example, Google Analytics or Cloudflare Web Analytics)

We do not use advertising cookies or retargeting pixels. We do not build advertising profiles based on your browsing activity.

Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies through your browser settings. Blocking cookies may affect your ability to use certain website features such as form submissions.

06 Third-Party Services & Tools

To provide our services, we may use third-party tools and platforms. These may include:

  • Remote support platforms (for example, HelpWire, Microsoft Quick Assist) to conduct remote sessions
  • Email service providers (for example, Brevo, Gmail) to send communications
  • Form processors (for example, Formspree) to receive intake form submissions
  • Website hosting and CDN (for example, Cloudflare) to serve and protect our website
  • Analytics platforms (for example, Google Analytics, Cloudflare Analytics) to analyze website traffic
  • Payment processors to handle transactions securely

Each third-party service operates under its own privacy policy and terms of service. We select tools that align with reasonable privacy and security standards, but we do not control and are not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party providers. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services involved in your engagement with us.

07 Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share limited information in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers. We share information with third-party tools and platforms that help us deliver our services (as described in Section 6), limited to what is necessary for them to perform their function.
  • Legal requirements. We may disclose information if required to do so by law, regulation, subpoena, court order, or other legal process.
  • Protection of rights. We may disclose information when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others, or to investigate potential violations of our terms of service.
  • Business transfers. If Stonevell is acquired, merged, or sells substantially all of its assets, customer information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify affected customers before their information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

08 Data Retention

We retain your personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, maintain our business records, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

  • Contact and intake form submissions are retained for up to 24 months after the last interaction, unless an ongoing service relationship exists.
  • Billing and transaction records are retained as required by applicable tax and accounting regulations.
  • Website analytics data is retained in aggregate form and does not include personally identifiable information.

You may request deletion of your personal data at any time by contacting us (see Section 13).

09 Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the information we collect. These measures include encrypted communications (HTTPS/TLS), access controls on internal systems, and secure authentication practices.

However, no method of transmission over the internet and no method of electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you believe your information has been compromised in connection with our services, please contact us immediately.

10 Your Rights & Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access. Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction. Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion. Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Objection. Object to certain uses of your personal data.
  • Portability. Request your data in a commonly used, machine-readable format.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the information in Section 13 below. We will respond to valid requests within 30 days. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing a request.

Nevada residents have the right to opt out of the sale of their personal information. As stated above, we do not sell personal information.

11 Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under 13 without parental consent, we will take steps to delete that information promptly. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately.

12 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, services, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Continued use of our website or services after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
